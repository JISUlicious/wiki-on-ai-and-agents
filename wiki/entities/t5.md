# T5 (Text-to-Text Transfer Transformer)

T5 is the encoder-decoder [[transformer-architecture|Transformer]] introduced in [[t5-raffel-2019|"Exploring the Limits of Transfer Learning with a Unified Text-to-Text Transformer"]] (Raffel et al., JMLR 2020). At [[google-brain|Google]]. The defining idea: **frame every NLP task as text-to-text** — translation, classification, regression, span extraction, summarization — and train a single model that solves all of them by string-in, string-out.

## Architecture

- Standard encoder-decoder [[transformer-architecture|Transformer]]. Similar to the original [[transformer|Vaswani et al. 2017]] but with relative positional embeddings, modified layer norms, and other small tweaks.
- 5 publicly released sizes: T5-Small (60M), T5-Base (220M), T5-Large (770M), T5-3B, T5-11B.
- Pre-trained on the [[c4|C4 (Colossal Clean Crawled Corpus)]] with a **span-corruption** objective: randomly mask contiguous spans of input tokens and ask the decoder to produce the missing spans.
- Open-weight: checkpoints and code released on GitHub.

## Successors

- **mT5** (Xue et al. 2020): multilingual variant trained on 101 languages.
- **ByT5** (2021): byte-level variant.
- **Flan-T5** (Chung et al. 2022): T5 fine-tuned with [[flan-wei-2021|FLAN]] instruction-tuning data. The de-facto strong instruction-tuned open-weight encoder-decoder until Llama-class decoder-only models replaced it.
- **UL2** / **Switch Transformer** / **LongT5** — various extensions and ablations.

## Significance

T5 is the canonical encoder-decoder LLM of the pre-GPT-3 era. While the [[decoder-only-transformer|decoder-only]] paradigm ([[gpt-family|GPT family]]) eventually dominated for general-purpose models, T5 demonstrated that:

1. **Text-to-text unification works.** Every NLP task becomes a single uniform training signal.
2. **Encoder-decoder remains competitive** with decoder-only at the same scale on most NLP benchmarks — especially translation and summarization.
3. **Span corruption** is a strong alternative to autoregressive LM as a pretraining objective.

T5's open-weight release and the C4 corpus also made it the default pretraining baseline for academic NLP work for several years.
