Jeff Clune is a professor at the [[university-of-british-columbia]] and faculty at the Vector Institute, with an advisory role at [[sakana-ai]]. He is a long-running figure in open-ended evolution, quality-diversity algorithms, novelty search, and AI-generating algorithms (AI-GAs). His research argues that the path to general intelligence runs through open-ended search systems that generate their own challenges, rather than purely scaled supervised learning.

Clune is senior author on the [[darwin-godel-machine]] paper, which combines his long-standing open-ended evolution agenda with modern LLM coding agents.

## Notable work

- [[darwin-godel-machine]] — senior-authored open-ended self-improving agent ([[darwin-godel-machine-zhang-2025]]).
- Earlier work on POET, quality-diversity, MAP-Elites, and novelty search.
- AI-GAs (AI-Generating Algorithms) research agenda.
