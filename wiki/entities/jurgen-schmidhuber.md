# Jürgen Schmidhuber

German computer scientist; co-author of "[[long-short-term-memory-hochreiter-1997|LSTM]]" (1997). Long-time scientific director at IDSIA (Lugano, Switzerland) and later faculty at KAUST. Prolific contributor to artificial neural network research since the 1980s — supervised learning, RL, meta-learning, GANs (he claims earlier work on adversarial nets), and curiosity-driven exploration. Known for vocal historical-priority claims on many ML ideas; the LSTM is the most universally credited.
