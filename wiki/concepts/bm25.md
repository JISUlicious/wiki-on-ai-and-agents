# BM25

BM25 (Best Matching 25; Robertson et al. 1994) is a classical sparse-vector retrieval algorithm based on a probabilistic relevance framework. It scores documents against a query as a sum, over query terms, of an IDF-weighted, length-normalized term-frequency function. It's the canonical strong baseline for [[dense-retrieval|dense retrieval]] papers including [[dpr-karpukhin-2020|DPR]].

For decades BM25 has been the workhorse of search engines (Lucene, Elasticsearch). Despite being non-neural and effectively training-free, it remains competitive — especially for queries dominated by exact-match cues (names, codes, jargon) — and is often combined with dense retrieval in hybrid systems.
