# Centaur Actor (Human-AI Hybrid)

A "centaur" is a composite actor that is neither purely human nor purely machine — a human or group of humans working in tight loop with one or more AI agents. The term comes from advanced chess (post-Deep Blue, "centaur chess" pairings of a human + a chess engine outperformed both alone) and has been generalized in the LLM era to any human-AI collaboration where the two are co-actors rather than tool-user-and-tool.

[[agentic-ai-and-the-next-intelligence-explosion|Evans, Bratton, & Agüera y Arcas (2026)]] argue that the **centaur is the canonical actor of the agentic AI era**:

> "Each one of us may move in and out of diverse ensembles many times a day: one human directing many AI agents; one AI serving many humans; many humans and many AIs collaborating in shifting configurations."

## Typology of centaurs

- **One human + many AIs**: e.g., a software engineer running multiple coding agents in parallel.
- **Many humans + one AI**: e.g., a customer-support team augmented by a shared assistant.
- **Mixed teams**: humans and AIs as peers in design, research, governance tasks.
- **Recursive / nested**: an AI agent that spawns sub-agents and coordinates them, while itself supervised by a human.

## Implications

- **Capability accounting becomes hard**: "Did the human or the AI do that?" is the wrong question. The centaur did it.
- **Skill and economic dynamics shift**: the high-value skill is *centaur composition* — knowing which AIs to deploy, how to evaluate their outputs, how to compose them.
- **Alignment is partly about the system, not just the model**: the question is not whether any individual AI is aligned but whether the human-AI ensemble produces aligned outputs.
