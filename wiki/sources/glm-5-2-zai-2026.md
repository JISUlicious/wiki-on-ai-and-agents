## Summary

GLM-5.2 is a **coding-first frontier model** with a **1M-token context window**, released open-weight under the MIT license (weights on Hugging Face under `zai-org`, week of June 16). Architecturally it reuses the GLM-5.1 base — a **744B-parameter [[mixture-of-experts|Mixture-of-Experts]]** model with **40B active parameters** — concentrating its changes in post-training for extended-context, long-horizon coding. Its headline systems contribution is **IndexShare**, a sparse-attention scheme that makes the 1M context practical.

## Key points

- **IndexShare sparse attention** — groups of four transformer layers **share one lightweight indexer** instead of each layer computing its own, cutting per-token attention cost (vendor: ~2.9× lower per-token FLOPs) for [[long-context-llm|long-context]] inference.
- **Scale** — 744B-param MoE, 40B active (same base as GLM-5.1); post-training retargeted at long-horizon coding/agentic tasks.
- **1M-token context**, coding-first positioning; competitive with closed-source leaders on coding "marathons" per third-party coverage.
- **Open weights, MIT license** — published under `zai-org` on Hugging Face; available via Z.ai and Zhipu's Coding Plan.
- **No published benchmarks** — ships without an official eval table or technical report.
