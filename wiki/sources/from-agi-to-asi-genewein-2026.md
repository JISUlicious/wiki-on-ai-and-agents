# From AGI to ASI

Surfaced via the alphaXiv weekly digest (2026-06-17).

## Summary

A Google DeepMind report that builds an analytical framework for how machine intelligence might continue to develop *after* human-level AGI is reached, mapping the transition from AGI to artificial superintelligence (ASI). The authors anchor the discussion in the well-understood theoretical limit of machine intelligence — Universal AI / AIXI — to give the otherwise speculative endpoint some formal grounding, then characterize ASI as a system more capable than large organizations of humans (while stressing it is neither omniscient nor omnipotent, being bound by physical and complexity-theoretic limits).

The core contribution is a structured research agenda rather than new empirical results. The report lays out four candidate technological pathways from AGI to ASI, the frictions and bottlenecks that could slow or halt progress along each, and the open research questions whose answers determine whether those frictions are negligible or decisive. The authors argue a single "step-change" picture of AGI arrival may be misleading; a series of compounding transformative changes is more apt, demanding broad interdisciplinary preparation.

## Key points

- Four pathways from AGI to ASI: (1) scaling AGI, (2) AI paradigm shifts, (3) recursive self-improvement, and (4) ASI emerging from large-scale multi-agent collectives — treated as non-exclusive and possibly parallel.
- Uses the AIXI / Universal AI framework as a formal upper bound on machine intelligence, complementing bottom-up extrapolation from today's systems and trends.
- Compute scaling is examined quantitatively: ~1.5x/yr hardware cost-efficiency for six decades, ~2.5x/yr investment growth, ~4x/yr total compute on largest training runs.
- ASI is bounded by fundamental limits (speed of light, Landauer principle, Bremermann's limit, Bekenstein bound) and by real-time constraints on experimentation.
- Several frictions/bottlenecks are catalogued per pathway; determining their impact is framed as the report's central set of open research questions.
- Conclusion: progress may keep accelerating, so prepare for a series of transformative societal changes rather than one discrete jump.
