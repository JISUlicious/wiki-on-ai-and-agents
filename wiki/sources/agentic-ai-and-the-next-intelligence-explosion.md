# Agentic AI and the Next Intelligence Explosion

**Source**: `sources/Agentic AI and the next intelligence explosion.md` (Obsidian Web Clipper output)
**arXiv**: [2603.20639v1](https://arxiv.org/html/2603.20639v1)
**Published**: April 2026
**Authors**: [[james-evans]] (University of Chicago, Santa Fe Institute), Benjamin Bratton (Antikythera, UCSD, Berggruen Institute), [[blaise-aguera-y-arcas]] (Paradigms of Intelligence Team, Google) — all of [[google-research|Google]]'s **Paradigms of Intelligence Team**.

## Summary

An essay-format argument that the "intelligence explosion" framing inherited from Vernor Vinge and Ray Kurzweil — a single mind bootstrapping to godlike intelligence — is fundamentally wrong. The authors argue instead for a **plural, social** view: intelligence has always been a collective property, and the AI version of the intelligence explosion will be a **combinatorial society** of human and non-human agents complexifying together, not a monolithic singularity.

Two empirical anchors:

1. **"Society of thought" inside reasoning models.** The authors cite a study (Kim et al. 2026 self-referentially) showing that reasoning models like DeepSeek-R1 and QwQ-32B don't improve simply by "thinking longer" but by simulating **multi-agent-like internal conversations** within their chain of thought — distinct cognitive perspectives that argue, verify, and reconcile. This emerges from RL training on accuracy rewards alone, suggesting that **social reasoning is rediscovered by optimization**.
2. **"Centaur" configurations.** Humans and AI agents combine into composite actors: one human directing many AIs, one AI serving many humans, mixed teams in shifting configurations. They cite "OpenClaw" and "Moltbook" as embryonic examples of agent platforms (the latter described as a social network for AI agents).

The essay's normative payload:

- **Institutional alignment, not just RLHF.** [[rlhf|RLHF]] is "fundamentally dyadic" (parent-child) and won't scale to billions of agents. Drawing on Ostrom and North, the authors argue for **institutional templates** — roles, norms, protocols (judge / attorney / jury, separation of powers, regulatory checks) that work independently of any individual occupant. This is positioned as the scalable alignment strategy.
- **Constitutional structure for AI governance.** AI systems with explicit, distinct values (transparency, equity, due process) should check and balance each other across government branches and between public and private sectors. Example: a labor-department AI auditing a corporation's hiring algorithm.
- **Governance also in the cybernetic sense.** Agent-to-agent and human-agent systems need built-in deliberation protocols, delegation scaffolds, conflict-resolution mechanisms — agent governance as protocol design.

## Key Points

- **Intelligence is plural and social, not monolithic.** Primate intelligence scaled with group size (Dunbar); humans got the "cultural ratchet" (Tomasello); writing/law/bureaucracy externalized social intelligence into institutions. AI extends this sequence.
- **LLMs as compressed social intelligence**: every parameter is "a compressed residue of communicative exchange." What migrates to silicon is social intelligence, not abstract reasoning per se.
- **Reasoning models simulate multi-agent debate internally** — "society of thought."
- **Conflict as a resource**, not a bug: hierarchy, specialization, division of labor, structured disagreement are blueprints from team-science and small-group sociology that should inform AI architecture.
- **Centaur paradigm**: hybrid human-AI actors, not "either/or."
- **Recursive agent societies**: agents fork into sub-agents that fork further, forming a folding/unfolding hypergraph of conversations. Spawn when complexity demands, collapse when resolved.
- **Institutional alignment > RLHF**: scaffold roles/norms rather than try to bake values into individual model weights.
- **Constitutional AI governance**: AI agents in adversarial / oversight roles across institutional boundaries.

## Notable Quotes

> "By its nature, intelligence is high-dimensional and relational, not a single quantity that must be unambiguously less or greater than human scale." — Introduction

> "When reinforcement learning is used to reward base models solely for reasoning accuracy, they spontaneously increase conversational, multi-perspective behaviors. Models are rediscovering, through optimization pressure alone, what centuries of epistemology and decades of cognitive science have suggested: that robust reasoning is a social process, even when it occurs within a single mind." — On "society of thought"

> "The dominant paradigm for AI alignment—Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback—resembles a parent-child model of correction, fundamentally dyadic and unable to scale to billions of agents. The social intelligence perspective suggests an alternative: institutional alignment." — On scaling alignment

> "The intelligence explosion is already here—in the society of thought debating inside every reasoning model, in the centaur workflows reshaping every knowledge profession, in the recursive agent ecologies beginning to fork and collaborate at scale, and in the constitutional questions we must now begin to ask." — Conclusion
