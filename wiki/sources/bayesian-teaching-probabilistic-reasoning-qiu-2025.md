# Bayesian Teaching Enables Probabilistic Reasoning in Large Language Models

[arXiv:2503.17523](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.17523) · Qiu, Sha, Allen, Kim, Linzen, van Steenkiste (MIT / Meta / Google DeepMind / Google Research / NYU) · 2025. Surfaced via the alphaXiv / NLP-newsletter digest.

## Summary
LLMs increasingly act as agents that must form and update probabilistic beliefs about users and the world (e.g., inferring preferences across multiple interactions). The paper measures how far LLMs fall short of normative Bayesian belief-updating using a controlled multi-round flight-recommendation task where the exact Bayesian strategy ("Bayesian Assistant") can be computed. Out of the box, LLMs perform far worse than the Bayesian Assistant and, unlike it, show little-to-no improvement as more information about the user accumulates across rounds.

The core contribution is "Bayesian teaching": supervised fine-tuning on interaction traces generated by the normative Bayesian model. This dramatically improves LLMs' belief-updating, and — crucially — the acquired skill generalizes to new tasks and domains beyond the one used for fine-tuning. The takeaway is a general strength of deep models: they can mimic a symbolic/normative model and transfer its strategy to settings where implementing exact Bayesian inference is intractable.

## Key points
- Diagnostic setup: a flight-booking task with multiple rounds; the LLM only observes the user's past choices and must infer latent preferences, enabling exact comparison to Bayes-optimal updates.
- Finding: all evaluated LLMs underperform the Bayesian Assistant and mostly stagnate after round 1 (no meaningful accumulation), unlike humans who improve with more information.
- Follow-up prompting/instruction interventions failed to fix the deficit, motivating fine-tuning.
- Method: SFT on traces from the Bayesian Assistant (and an oracle variant) teaches LLMs to mimic normative updating; the Bayesian teacher is more effective than an oracle teacher, especially in early rounds.
- Result: fine-tuning yields large, statistically significant gains and generalizes to two new held-out tasks.
- Implication: LLMs can absorb reasoning skills from a symbolic teacher and transfer them, useful where exact Bayesian computation is infeasible.
